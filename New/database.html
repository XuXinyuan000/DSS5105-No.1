Overview of ESG Scores

The chart illustrates the performance of various companies included in our database in terms of their Total ESG Score

Each company's score is based on a comprehensive evaluation of three dimensions: Environmental, Social, and Governance. The score ranges from 0 to 10, reflecting the company's overall strength in the field of sustainability.

  • Low Performer: BlackRock scored only 1
  • Mid-Level: China Life, HSBC scored 5-6

Social Score

This chart illustrates the Social Scores (S Score) of various companies, ranging from 0 to 10 points

  • However, there is still room for improvement for a few companies with lower scores.

Governance Score

This chart illustrates the Governance Scores (G Score) of various companies, with a scale ranging from 0 to 10

  • Governance scores are generally low across all companies, with the highest scores only nearing 4 points, highlighting governance as a weaker area in ESG performance.
  • Governance is a critical component of ESG performance, and companies should prioritize it as a key area for development.

Rating and Score Distribution

We categorize companies' ESG scores, ranging from 0 to 10, into seven distinct levels.

  • Low-Rated Companies (B, CCC): Improve sustainability metrics, governance, and transparency.
  • Mid-Rated Companies (A, BBB, BB): Strengthen current practices and address weaknesses.
  • High-Rated Companies (AAA, AA): Maintain strengths and innovate in sustainability.

Accuracy of Model

Evaluated through Cross-Validation, this result fully demonstrates the model's capability in data extraction and feature learning

The accuracy indicates that the model can correctly capture 84.72% of the patterns and structures in the test data, effectively extracting meaningful information from the raw data to achieve reliable predictions.

Scoring Formula

Our scoring formula was established with reference to MSCI's esg rating methodology.

Environmental accounts for 40% of the total score, while Social and Governance each account for 30%.
